The strategy

Before. During.
After.

Three moments, three goals. Nail all three and your legs feel the difference.

Before

Fill the tank

Carb-rich meal 2–3h before. On race days, cut fibre and fat to avoid surprises.

• 1–4 g carbs / kg
• 400–600 ml water
During

Hold the pace

Beyond 60–90 min, top up energy. Gels, sports drink or dried fruit every 30–40 minutes.

• 30–60 g carbs / h
• 400–800 ml fluid / h
After

Rebuild

30–60 min window: combine carbs and protein to refill glycogen and repair muscle.

• 1 g carbs / kg
• 20–30 g protein
Supplements, no myths

What's backed by
evidence.

You don't need a full shelf. These are the supplements with real scientific support for runners.

Caffeine

Strong evidence

3–6 mg/kg, 45–60 min before. Lowers perceived effort and improves performance in long races.

Carb gels

Strong evidence

The most practical way to refuel in a race. Train your gut first — nothing new on race day.

Salts / electrolytes

Context

Useful in long efforts, heat or if you sweat a lot. Sodium matters most — don't overdo it.

Protein (whey)

Support

Helps you hit daily needs and recover. Real food comes first.

Informational content. Consult a sports nutritionist before changing your diet or supplements.
